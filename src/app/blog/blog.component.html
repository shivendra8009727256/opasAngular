Climate change and grain trade

10/04/2025

Climate change is reshaping global agriculture, with profound implications for grain production, trade, and food security. Rising temperatures, erratic rainfall, and extreme weather events—such as droughts, floods, and heatwaves—are disrupting traditional growing cycles, reducing yields, and altering trade flows. The grain trade, a $200+ billion industry, is particularly vulnerable. Major exporters like the U.S., Brazil, Russia, and Argentina face climate-induced challenges, while import-dependent regions (Africa, the Middle East, and Asia) grapple with supply shortages and price volatility.
